# Future Product Capabilities

> **Status: Living document.** A permanent, evolving record of product capabilities accepted as valuable but
> **intentionally deferred** to future implementation. Updated as capabilities are accepted or shipped.

> ActivLife Hub aims to become the primary operational platform for event organizers. Whenever practical,
> organizers should be able to complete their work inside ActivLife Hub without relying on multiple
> disconnected applications.

## What this document is (and is not)

This document is **not**:

- architecture;
- an implementation plan;
- a roadmap;
- a backlog.

It is a permanent record of important product capabilities that should **not be forgotten** as development
continues — a place to keep accepted-but-deferred ideas safe so they resurface at the right time.

## Long-term direction

The long-term direction is to **reduce organizers' dependence on fragmented tools** — messaging apps,
spreadsheets, standalone document systems, task managers, registration tools, and similar operational
software — by providing **integrated workflows inside ActivLife Hub**. Work that today is scattered across
many disconnected applications should, over time, be doable in one place, connected to the same Project.

## Priority

Acceptance here does **not** mean every capability has equal priority. Many capabilities are **intentionally
deferred** until the core platform is complete. This document records *what* is valuable, not *when* it is
built; sequencing lives in the roadmap, not here.

## Accepted future capabilities

Capabilities accepted as strategically valuable and intentionally deferred (illustrative, not exhaustive):

- **Event photo collection through participant QR codes** — participants contribute photos to the Project via
  a QR code, feeding the after-activity memory.
- **Expanded organizer document automation** — generating and managing more of the organizer's operational
  documents inside ALH.
- **Additional collaboration tools** — richer coordination for teams, vendors, and helpers around a Project.
- **Future integrations where appropriate** — connecting to external services when it reduces organizer load
  rather than adding fragmentation.
- **Other accepted capabilities already documented elsewhere** — capabilities recorded in existing specs,
  proposals, or principles but deferred (e.g. the business-layer capabilities in the Organizer Business
  proposal, and later-stage Project Runtime capabilities) are considered part of this record by reference.

*New accepted-but-deferred capabilities are added here as they are agreed.*

---

*Acceptance into this document means the capability is considered strategically valuable. It does not imply
