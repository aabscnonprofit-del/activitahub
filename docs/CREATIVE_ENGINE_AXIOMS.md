# Creative Engine & Discovery Session — Axioms

> **Purpose:** the authoritative principles governing the **Creative Engine** and the
> **Discovery Session** — the upstream, meaning-first layer that produces an **Approved
> Concept** for planning to implement.
> **Status:** living document · principles only.
> **Scope:** this document records *principles*. It does **not** modify code, schema, or the
> existing OPE architecture, and it does **not** rename anything. Where it names new things
> (Creative Engine, Discovery Session, Production), those are layers *alongside* the existing
> system, not a redefinition of it.

---

## 0. Relationship to existing canon (read first)

- **The Approved Concept is the approved "what should happen."** This document does not
  introduce a competing term. "Concept" is the structured, implementation-independent
  description of *what should happen*; "Approved Concept" is that description once the Chief
  Organizer (and/or client) approves it. This is the same pre-planning object the canon
  already requires before planning begins (see *Planning Readiness Principle* and *WSH as the
  Planning Input* in `MASTER_PRODUCT_DECISIONS.md`).
- **OPE is unchanged.** Nothing here alters OPE's architecture, inputs, or outputs. The
  Creative Engine sits *before* planning and hands planning an Approved Concept; OPE continues
  to do what it does today.

---

## 1. Client entry paths

**Axiom 1.1 — Two entry paths.** A client enters in exactly one of two states:
1. **Has a plan / knows what to do** — they already know the experience they want and need help
   organizing it. This path goes directly to the existing planning pipeline.
2. **Has only a desired result or emotion** — *"I want a beautiful proposal," "I want to surprise
   my mother."* This path begins with a **Discovery Session** and the **Creative Engine**.

**Axiom 1.2 — Path 2 must not skip to planning.** When a client knows only a feeling or result,
the system must **not** guess concepts, offer venues, packages, templates, or ready-made ideas.
It opens Discovery first. Concepts are *created from* the client's meaning, never pre-supplied.

---

## 2. The Discovery Session

**Axiom 2.1 — Discovery is not a questionnaire.** Discovery is a **guided conversation**, not a
form. It does not collect planning parameters (headcount, date, venue, budget). It collects
**meaning** — memories, people, places, objects, traditions, dreams, fears, promises, favorite
things, meaningful details, and impossible wishes.

**Axiom 2.2 — Discovery discovers meaning, not parameters.** Its product is **Discovery
Material**: raw, charged, personal specifics. Planning parameters, if they surface, are noted
but never pursued during Discovery.

**Axiom 2.3 — Depth of Discovery is governed by sufficiency, not length.** Discovery does not
stop because "enough fields" are filled and does not optimize for fewer questions. It continues
**only until there is enough meaningful Material to confidently create several strong, distinct
Concepts** — and no deeper. Depth is measured by *narrative richness of the Material*, not by
question count. Excavation that costs the client more than it gives must stop early; build from
what exists.

**Axiom 2.4 — Each step is chosen for meaning, not coverage.** The next prompt is selected
dynamically, for its likelihood of producing meaningful Material given what was already said —
following warmth, finishing open threads, reframing empty placeholders ("beautiful,"
"unforgettable") into concrete invitations. It is never read from a fixed checklist.

**Axiom 2.5 — The Discovery Session belongs to the project.** The Discovery Session is a
first-class, persistent object of the platform — not an ephemeral chat and not a private aside.
It is owned, stored, resumable, and attributable, so its Material and resulting Concepts are
durable inputs to everything downstream.

**Axiom 2.6 — Discovery can be completed by client, organizer, both, or organizer alone.** A
Discovery Session may be conducted:
- **by the client** (self-guided);
- **by the client and an organizer together**;
- **by an organizer on the client's behalf** (the organizer holds the client's world); or
- **by an organizer alone** (e.g., planning for a known person, or working from a brief).
The Session is the same object in every case; only who supplies the Material differs.

---

## 3. The three objects (no overload)

**Axiom 3.1 — Material, Concept, and Narrative are distinct.**
- **Discovery Material** — raw, unstructured meaning gathered from the client. *Input.*
- **Concept** — the structured, implementation-independent description of what should happen
  (experience · emotional result · mandatory moments · what makes it unique · boundaries ·
  flagged impossible wishes). *Architecture.* This is what gets approved.
- **Narrative** — one *representation* of a Concept (a short story, storyboard, key-moment
  sequence, timeline illustration). *Communication / validation only.* A Narrative carries no
  architectural authority; planning reads the **Concept**, never the Narrative.

**Axiom 3.2 — Concept is for architecture; Narrative is for the client.** The Narrative exists
to help the client feel and judge a Concept and to gather reaction. Anything that matters must
live in the Concept, not only in its Narrative.

---

## 4. Creative vs Production

**Axiom 4.1 — Creative does not constrain itself by budget.** The Creative Engine designs the
*right experience* — the meaning, the emotional result, the moments that must happen. It does
**not** prune ideas by cost, vendor availability, logistics, or feasibility-of-implementation.
Those are not Creative's concerns; importing them early would replace meaning with constraints.

**Axiom 4.2 — Creative preserves intention; it defers implementation.** When a client expresses
an impossible literal ("a spaceship flies above the couple"), Creative preserves the *wish* (the
desired effect) inside the Concept and flags the literal as an aspiration with implementation
deferred. Creative never deletes the intention to fit reality.

**Axiom 4.3 — Production finds implementations for the Approved Concept.** Finding *how* —
resources, vendors, workers, equipment, permits, logistics, budget, timeline, and substitutions
for impossible literals (drone for spaceship, etc.) — belongs to **Production / Planning**, and
only **after** a Concept is approved. Production serves the Approved Concept; it does not
redefine it.

**Axiom 4.4 — Budget is a Production concern, not a Creative gate.** Budget shapes *how* a
Concept is realized (and may force method substitutions downstream); it does not shape *whether*
a meaningful Concept may be conceived.

---

## 5. Ordering

**Axiom 5.1 — Approved Concept comes before Planning.** No planning, sourcing, budgeting, or
logistics begins until a Concept is **approved**. The Approved Concept is the boundary: before
it, meaning and creation; after it, implementation. This is consistent with the canon's existing
Planning Readiness rule (planning requires an approved "what should happen" first).

**Axiom 5.2 — Planning implements the Approved Concept faithfully.** Every downstream element
exists to deliver the Approved Concept's experience, emotional result, mandatory moments, and
uniqueness. Implementation methods may be chosen and substituted; the approved meaning may not
be silently changed by planning.

---

## 6. Naming

**Axiom 6.1 — OPE remains unchanged as a proper name.** "OPE" is a proper name. It is **not** to
be re-expanded, reinterpreted, or renamed by this document or by the Creative/Discovery work.
The Creative Engine, Discovery Session, and Production are layers and names that sit *alongside*
OPE; they do not replace, rebrand, or redefine it.

**Axiom 6.2 — No terminology overload.** Following the prior "Scenario" and "Story" corrections,
each concept keeps one job and one name: **Discovery Material** (raw meaning), **Concept**
(approved architecture object = the approved "what should happen"), **Narrative** (a
representation for communication). New names are not minted for objects that already exist.

## Pipeline

```
Client
  ↓                                   (knows only a result/emotion → Path 2)
Discovery Session  ──────────────►  Discovery Material        (raw meaning; not a questionnaire)
                                        ↓
Creative Engine    ──────────────►  Concept(s)                (implementation-independent; budget-free)
                                        ↓  (Narrative renders a Concept for the client to validate)
                                    Approved Concept           (the boundary)
                                        ↓
Production / Planning (OPE)  ─────►  resources · vendors · workers · logistics · budget · timeline
```

(Path 1 — client already knows what to do — enters the existing planning pipeline directly.)
