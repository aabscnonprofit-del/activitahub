# Planning Axioms

> **Status: AUTHORITATIVE (architectural axioms).** This document records **only** the foundational
> architectural axioms of Planning — the statements from which later Planning behavior will naturally follow.
> It is **not** a Product Behavior Specification and **not** a Technical Design. It contains no implementation,
> UI, database, or algorithm detail, and introduces no new concept. Every axiom restates a principle already
> established during the Planning research and the accepted architecture (the accepted pipeline, Canon §10–§11,
> `FUTURE_EVENT_DESCRIPTION_SPEC.md`, `AI_ARTIFACT_OWNERSHIP_PRINCIPLE.md`, and the pipeline approval/revision
> gates). Where this document and those disagree, those documents win.

---

## Scope

1. **Planning cannot exist without an approved Future Event Description — it is Planning's mandatory prerequisite.**

2. **Planning realizes the approved Future Event Description. It never invents, extends, or redesigns it.**

3. **Planning determines what must become reality, and when it must become reality.**

## The organizing center

4. **Time is the organizing axis of Planning.**

5. **A planned reality is part of the plan only insofar as it is reachable within the available time and means.** Feasibility is where finite means first constrain the plan.

6. **Traditional planning concepts — tasks, resources, budget, vendors, logistics, communications — are derived from Planning, not what defines it.** They exist only to realize the planned reality.

## Production, iteration, and approval

7. **Planning is produced by its dedicated Planning intelligence.** If that intelligence cannot produce the Planning result, Planning fails and nothing is fabricated in its place.

8. **Planning builds the first version automatically.**

9. **Planning is iterative.** The organizer may introduce constraints and corrections. Planning automatically recalculates the plan after each accepted change.

10. **Planning completes only after explicit organizer approval.** The AI performs the work; the organizer decides.

## Downstream

11. **Only an approved Planning result may enter the Project Workspace.**

12. **If the underlying intent changes, the workflow returns to the most recent approved stage.** Previously approved results remain valid unless that intent changes.
