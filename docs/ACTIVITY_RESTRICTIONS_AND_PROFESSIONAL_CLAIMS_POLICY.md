# Activity Restrictions & Professional Claims Policy

> **Purpose:** MVP platform rules on professional claims, educational/coaching
> activities, organizer responsibility, and platform liability boundaries.
> **Status:** ✅ Decided — MVP policy of record.
> **Date:** 2026-06-13
> **Scope:** Applies to all activities organized, planned, or published through
> ActivLife Hub during the MVP. No verification, badge, or trust mechanism is
> introduced by this policy.

> Brand note: the product brand is **ActivLife Hub**. This policy governs activity
> content and claims; it does not change architecture, gating, or onboarding.

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy keeps organizers free to create the widest range of real-world
activities while preventing **false or misleading professional claims** that could
harm participants or expose the platform. It sets, for the MVP, who is responsible
for an activity, what may and may not be claimed, and where ActivLife Hub's role
ends. It deliberately avoids licensing checks, credential verification, or trust
scoring — those are out of scope for the MVP (see §8).

## 2. General Rule

Organizers may create educational, coaching, networking, entertainment, fitness,
hobby, community, and personal-development activities **without license verification**
in the MVP. The single hard rule: an activity and its description must **not claim
or imply a regulated professional service** (medical, psychological, legal,
financial, or similar) unless the organizer is genuinely qualified and personally
responsible for that claim. When in doubt, describe the *experience*, not a
*professional outcome*.

## 3. Professional Claims

- An organizer **must not present** an activity as regulated professional advice,
  diagnosis, treatment, therapy, legal counsel, or financial advice unless they are
  actually qualified to provide it and accept full responsibility for doing so.
- Prohibited framings include (non-exhaustive): "medical treatment," "therapy
  session," "guaranteed cure," "licensed legal advice," "investment/financial
  advice," "psychological diagnosis."
- Permitted framings include: education, coaching, mentoring, skill practice,
  workshops, discussion, networking, support communities, and general information —
  described as such.
- ActivLife Hub does **not** verify qualifications in the MVP. The absence of
  verification does **not** grant permission to make false claims; the
  responsibility and consequences rest entirely with the organizer (see §6).

## 4. Educational, Coaching & Personal Development Activities

These activities are **explicitly welcome**. Coaches, instructors, facilitators,
and community leaders may run classes, workshops, coaching, and personal-development
sessions freely. They must, however:

- Frame the activity as educational/coaching/informational — not as a regulated
  professional service (§3).
- Avoid promising clinical, legal, financial, or medical outcomes.
- Include the required disclaimer (§5) where the activity could be mistaken for
  professional advice.

This preserves organizer freedom (a core platform value) while drawing a clear line
at false professional claims.

## 5. Required Disclaimer

Where an activity is educational, coaching, networking, entertainment, or
personal-development in nature — and especially where it could be mistaken for
professional advice — the organizer must include this disclaimer in the activity
description:

> "This activity is intended for educational, informational, networking,
> entertainment, or personal development purposes only and does not constitute
> medical, psychological, legal, financial, or other professional advice."

The organizer is responsible for displaying an accurate disclaimer. ActivLife Hub
may surface this text as suggested/standard copy but does not police its presence in
the MVP.

## 6. Activity Responsibility

> "The organizer is solely responsible for the activity, its content, participant
> safety, compliance with local laws, permits, licenses, venue requirements, and
> any professional claims made in connection with the activity.
>
> ActivLife Hub provides planning, coordination, discovery, and organizational
> tools only."

## 7. Platform Position

ActivLife Hub is a planning, coordination, discovery, and organizational platform —
**not** a provider, employer, supervisor, or guarantor of any activity. It does not
endorse organizers, validate claims, or assume responsibility for activity content,
outcomes, safety, or legality. Liability for the activity and its claims remains
with the organizer. This position is consistent with the MVP's no-verification,
no-badge, no-trust-scoring stance and introduces no new platform mechanism.

## 8. Future Expansion

Items intentionally **deferred** beyond the MVP (tracked, not decided here):

- Credential / license verification for regulated activity categories.
- Category-specific gating or required documentation.
- Trust, badge, or reputation signals tied to professional standing.
- Automated detection or enforcement of disclaimer presence and prohibited claims.
- Region-specific legal/compliance requirements.

Until then, this policy stands on **organizer responsibility + a clear disclaimer**,
preserving organizer freedom while preventing false professional claims.
