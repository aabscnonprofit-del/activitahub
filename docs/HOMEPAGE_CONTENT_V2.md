# Homepage Content v2 — ActivLife Hub

> **Purpose:** the full content structure (copy + positioning) for the four priority homepage sections.
> **Type:** content/messaging design. No code, no mockups. Focus: positioning, messaging, conversion.
> **Sources:** `MASTER_PRODUCT_DECISIONS.md`, `BRAND_FOUNDATION_AND_PLATFORM_STRUCTURE.md`,
> `WEBSITE_IMPLEMENTATION_BACKLOG_V1.md`.
> **Date:** 2026-06-06

**Guardrails applied:**
- **Order (Master §11.7):** user-first hook, organizer opportunity unmistakable on the second level →
  flow: Hero → Mission → Become an Organizer → Academy.
- **Mission & philosophy (§11.1–§11.2):** "The World Needs More Organizers" + "People create the best
  moments of real life. We help."
- **All-paid (§11.8):** **no "Free to start / No credit card required"** language anywhere. Lead with
  value, not "free." (No specific prices on the homepage — link to Pricing.)
- **OPE is the differentiator (§11.6, §10.3):** organizers look professional from day one.
- **Success = First Client → First Event → First Income (§10.4):** certification is a milestone, not
  the goal.
- **Trust (§3):** Certified (skill) and, where applicable, Verified (identity) organizers.

Each section provides: **Headline · Subheadline · Supporting text · CTA buttons · Key messages**,
plus a short *positioning intent* and *conversion goal*.

---

## 1. Hero Section

*Positioning intent:* a warm, human, user-first welcome that carries mission energy and makes the
organizer opportunity visible immediately. *Conversion goal:* route everyone to a next step — explore
activities (demand) or start the organizer path (supply).

- **Headline:** **"The best moments in life are made by someone."**
  - *Alternates:* "Real life happens when someone brings people together." · "Great gatherings don't
    just happen — someone makes them."
- **Subheadline:** "Discover activities near you, plan your own event, or become the organizer your
  community is waiting for."
- **Tagline (retain):** *Activate Life Together.*
- **Supporting text:** "ActivLife Hub connects people who want real experiences with trusted, certified
  organizers — and helps more people turn their gift for gathering others into a profession."
- **CTA buttons:**
  - **Primary (user):** "Explore activities" → marketplace
  - **Secondary (organizer):** "Become an organizer" → Become-an-Organizer / Academy
- **Reassurance line (replaces "Free to start"):** "Certified organizers · Secure payments · Available
  in 4 languages."
- **Key messages:**
  1. Moments are *created* by people — not automatic.
  2. Two clear ways in: take part / plan an event (demand) or organize as a profession (supply).
  3. Trust is built in (certified organizers, secure payments).
  4. The organizer opportunity is real and present from the first screen.

---

## 2. Mission Section — "The World Needs More Organizers"

*Positioning intent:* state the brand's reason to exist; elevate the page from "marketplace" to a
movement. *Conversion goal:* create emotional buy-in that primes the Become-an-Organizer section.

- **Headline:** **"The World Needs More Organizers."**
- **Subheadline:** "People create the best moments of real life. We help."
- **Supporting text:** "Friendship, community, and celebration don't happen on their own — someone sends
  the first message, books the space, and carries the small risks so everyone else can simply show up
  and belong. The world has far fewer organizers than it needs. We exist to change that: to make it
  dramatically easier to become one — and, for those who want it, to turn that ability into a
  profession. You are the hero of every gathering. We're the help behind the scenes."
- **CTA buttons:**
  - **Primary:** "Become an organizer" → Become-an-Organizer
  - **Secondary (text link):** "Read our mission" → About / Mission page
- **Key messages:**
  1. Organizers are the quiet infrastructure of real life.
  2. There aren't enough of them — and that's the problem we solve.
  3. The human is the hero; ActivLife Hub is the helper, never the protagonist.
  4. Becoming an organizer can be a profession, not just a hobby.

---

## 3. Become an Organizer Section

*Positioning intent:* convert the **primary audience** (aspiring organizers — career changers, side
income, returning to work, community builders) by selling **profession + income**, made achievable by
OPE. *Conversion goal:* start the organizer path (onboarding/Academy).

- **Headline:** **"Turn your ability to bring people together into a profession."**
  - *Alternate:* "From your first lesson to your first paid event."
- **Subheadline:** "A clear path — learn the craft, get certified, win your first client, and earn —
  with tools that make you look professional from day one."
- **Supporting text:** "Whether you're changing careers, building a side income, returning to work, or
  already the person who organizes everything for everyone — ActivLife Hub gives you the system around
  your enthusiasm. Learn in the Academy. Earn the Certified Organizer credential that earns clients'
  trust. Then let **OPE**, our planning engine, turn a few details into a professional client proposal —
  with a timeline, plan, and budget — in minutes instead of hours. Real clients are already looking for
  organizers in the marketplace; your job is to say yes."
- **The path (key visual/copy beats):** **Learn → Get Certified → First Client → First Event → First
  Income.**
- **CTA buttons:**
  - **Primary:** "Start your organizer path" → onboarding
  - **Secondary:** "See how it works" → Become-an-Organizer page
- **Key messages:**
  1. This is a **profession and an income**, not just hosting (Master §10.1).
  2. There's a **defined path**, not a leap — you always know your next step.
  3. **OPE** removes the "I don't know where to start / I'll look like an amateur" barrier (§11.6).
  4. **Real demand** exists (Event Requests / marketplace) — you answer it.
  5. **Success = your first client, event, and income** — certification is a milestone on the way (§10.4).

---

## 4. Organizer Academy Section

*Positioning intent:* make certification credible and explorable *before* sign-up; show what's taught
and what the credential unlocks. *Conversion goal:* enter the Academy (explore) or begin certification.

- **Headline:** **"Learn the craft. Earn the credential."**
  - *Alternate:* "Become a Certified Organizer."
- **Subheadline:** "The ActivLife Hub Academy turns enthusiasm into a certified profession — two ways
  in, one recognized credential."
- **Supporting text:** "New to organizing? Take the **beginner course** and learn it step by step.
  Already experienced? Prove it with the **fast-track skills test**. Either way you learn what actually
  makes events work — planning and timelines, vendors and budgets, handling clients, and running the day
  with confidence — and you earn the **Certified Organizer** credential clients can verify. (For added
  trust, organizers can also become **Verified** — confirming who they are, alongside what they can do.)
  The Academy is a paid program — an investment in a real profession, not a free trial."
- **What you'll learn (curriculum beats):** planning & timelines · vendors & resources · budgeting &
  pricing · client communication · running the event · trust & safety standards.
- **CTA buttons:**
  - **Primary:** "Explore the Academy" → Academy landing
  - **Secondary:** "Get certified" → onboarding
- **Key messages:**
  1. **Two entry paths:** beginner course or experienced fast-track test.
  2. **Real, practical curriculum** — not theory.
  3. The **Certified Organizer** credential is verifiable and earns client trust; **Verified** is a
     separate identity badge (§3).
  4. It's a **paid program** (no free tier) — framed as investing in a profession (§11.8).
  5. Certification opens the door; the goal beyond it is **clients and income** (§10.4).

## Notes for implementation (content-level only)

- **Flow & balance:** Hero welcomes users (demand) and surfaces the organizer path; Sections 2–4 build
  the organizer case (supply). This satisfies the user-first / organizer-second rule (§11.7).
- **Monetization voice:** never imply a free core product; replace any "free to start" with
  value/trust lines. Defer numbers to the Pricing page.
- **OPE claims:** keep proportional to build status — "create professional proposals in minutes" is the
  promise; avoid demoing features not yet shipped (Backlog BL-05 dependency).
- **i18n:** all copy ships in en/es/fr/ru.
- **Out of scope here (separate sections/pages):** Marketplace discovery grid, Activity Planner front
  door, Trust & Safety, Pricing — see `WEBSITE_IMPLEMENTATION_BACKLOG_V1.md`.
