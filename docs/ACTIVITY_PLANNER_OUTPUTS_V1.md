# Example 1 — Kids Birthday Party

## A. What you told us
- **Planning:** Kids birthday party
- **Guests:** ~15 children (ages 6–7) + ~10 adults
- **Budget:** around $600
- **Location:** Backyard at home (outdoors)
- **Special requirements:** Superhero theme · one child has a **nut allergy** · mostly young kids

## B. Your Activity Plan

**Summary**
A 3-hour superhero backyard party for about 15 kids and 10 adults. The plan keeps energy high with a
simple flow — arrival & free play, themed games, food & cake, then a calm wind-down — and is built to
be safe for young children and easy for you to run with one or two helpers.

**Timeline**
- **3–4 weeks before:** lock date & time, send invites, decide theme details, book any entertainer.
- **1 week before:** confirm headcount, buy non-perishables & décor, plan the food, prep games.
- **1–2 days before:** shop for fresh food, bake/order cake, set up what you can, charge camera.
- **Day of:** decorate, set up food & activity stations, run the party, wind down, send guests home.

**Preparation tasks (checklist)**
- ☐ Pick a 3-hour window (early afternoon works best for this age)
- ☐ Send invitations with RSVP + ask about allergies (see templates)
- ☐ Confirm final headcount a few days out
- ☐ Plan the menu: simple kid food + an **allergy-safe, clearly-labeled** option
- ☐ Order or plan the cake (confirm **nut-free**)
- ☐ Buy décor, tableware, and 2–3 games' supplies
- ☐ Optional: book a face painter or entertainer
- ☐ Prepare a small first-aid kit and know the nut-allergy plan

**Day-of tasks**
- ☐ Decorate the yard + set up a shaded area
- ☐ Set up food table (keep allergy-safe items separate and labeled)
- ☐ Welcome kids; start with free play while everyone arrives
- ☐ Run 2–3 games, then food, then cake & candles
- ☐ Hand out party favors as kids leave

**After-event tasks**
- ☐ Pack up & clean the yard
- ☐ Send thank-you messages (see template)
- ☐ Note what worked for next time

## C. Budget estimate (illustrative)
| | Low | Likely | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total | **$350** | **$600** | **$950** |

**Key cost drivers:** food & drinks for kids and adults · birthday cake · decorations & tableware ·
entertainment (face painter / small magician) · party favors. *Skipping the entertainer is the easiest
way to move toward the low end; a hired entertainer + custom cake pushes toward the high end.*

## D. Risk reminders
- **Children:** for 15 young kids, aim for at least 2–3 adults actively supervising; keep an eye on the
  yard's boundaries and any pool/water.
- **Food:** one guest has a **nut allergy** — confirm the cake and snacks are nut-free, label the
  allergy-safe table, and tell helpers. Keep an allergy action plan handy.
- **Weather:** it's outdoors — set up shade, have sunscreen/water, and pick a simple indoor backup
  (garage/living room) in case of rain.
- **Attendance:** kids' RSVPs shift — confirm headcount 2–3 days before so you don't over- or
  under-cater.

## E. Ready-to-send messages
**Invitation**
> 🦸 You're invited to [Child]'s Superhero Birthday! 🎉
> When: [Date], [Time]–[Time] · Where: our backyard, [Address]
> Come in your favorite superhero costume! Please reply by [RSVP date], and let us know about any
> **food allergies** so we can keep everyone safe. Can't wait to see you!

**Reminder (2 days before)**
> Hi! Just a reminder that [Child]'s superhero party is this [Day] at [Time], at [Address]. We're
> excited to see [Guest]! Reply if anything's changed. 🦸

**Thank-you (after)**
> Thank you so much for celebrating [Child]'s birthday with us! 🎂 They had the best time. Thanks for
> coming (and for the lovely gift) — hope to see you again soon!

**Feedback request (optional)**
> So glad you could make it! If you have a sec — was the timing and setup good for the kids? Any tips
> for next year? 🙏

## F. Upgrade path
**You can absolutely run this yourself.** Consider hiring a professional organizer if: the guest list
grows past ~25–30 kids, you want a fully-hosted themed experience (entertainers, catering, setup &
cleanup handled), or you simply don't want to run it on the day.
**One tap turns this plan into a request** — your answers and this plan become a brief that local,
certified organizers can respond to with quotes. You keep the plan either way.

# Example 2 — BBQ / Family Picnic

## A. What you told us
- **Planning:** BBQ / family picnic
- **Guests:** ~30 (mixed ages, several kids)
- **Budget:** around $450
- **Location:** Public park (outdoors)
- **Special requirements:** Need **vegetarian options** · kids present · guests bring their own chairs

## B. Your Activity Plan

**Summary**
A relaxed 4-hour afternoon BBQ for ~30 people at the park. The plan covers permits, grilling logistics,
a crowd-pleasing menu with clear vegetarian options, a kids' play area, and an easy pack-down — designed
so a couple of organizers can run it comfortably.

**Timeline**
- **2 weeks before:** confirm date, reserve the park spot / check permit rules, invite guests.
- **1 week before:** confirm headcount & dietary needs, plan menu, line up grill + coolers, assign
  who-brings-what.
- **1–2 days before:** shop, prep marinades & sides, freeze ice packs, charge speaker.
- **Day of:** arrive early to claim the spot, set up, grill in batches, run the afternoon, pack out.

**Preparation tasks (checklist)**
- ☐ Check the park's rules: BBQ/open-flame allowed? permit needed? parking?
- ☐ Reserve a shaded spot near restrooms if possible
- ☐ Invite guests + collect dietary needs (see templates)
- ☐ Plan menu: mains (incl. **clearly separate vegetarian**), sides, drinks, dessert
- ☐ Arrange grill, fuel, coolers, water, shade (canopy/umbrellas)
- ☐ Assign a "bring list" (chairs, blankets, a side dish)
- ☐ Pack tongs, foil, trash bags, first-aid, hand sanitizer

**Day-of tasks**
- ☐ Arrive ~45 min early; claim & set up the area
- ☐ Set up a **separate veggie grilling zone / order** to avoid cross-contact
- ☐ Keep raw meat cold and away from ready food
- ☐ Grill in batches; keep drinks & water flowing
- ☐ Keep an eye on the kids' play area
- ☐ Pack out all trash; leave the spot clean

**After-event tasks**
- ☐ Return/clean borrowed gear (grill, coolers)
- ☐ Send thank-yous + share photos
- ☐ Settle any shared costs

## C. Budget estimate (illustrative)
| | Low | Likely | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total | **$250** | **$450** | **$700** |

**Key cost drivers:** food (meat + vegetarian) · drinks · charcoal/fuel or grill rental · ice & coolers
· disposables/serveware · optional canopy/shade rental · park permit (if required). *Potluck-style
"bring a dish" moves you toward the low end; rented canopy + fuller catering pushes the high end.*

## D. Risk reminders
- **Weather:** fully outdoors — bring shade, sunscreen, and plenty of water; set a **rain date** or a
  nearby covered backup.
- **Food:** handle raw meat safely (keep cold, separate boards/tongs), keep **vegetarian items
  separate**, and ask about other allergies on the RSVP.
- **Children:** assign someone to watch the play area; note the nearest water/road hazards in the park.
- **Transportation:** parking at parks fills up — suggest carpooling and share exact directions / the
  meeting spot.
- **Attendance:** outdoor RSVPs are weather-sensitive; confirm numbers the day before.

## E. Ready-to-send messages
**Invitation**
> ☀️🍔 Family BBQ & Picnic at [Park]!
> When: [Date], [Time]–[Time] · Where: [Park], near [landmark]
> Bring a chair/blanket and your appetite — we'll handle the grill! Reply by [date] with your headcount
> and any **dietary needs (we'll have vegetarian options)**. Kids welcome!

**Reminder (1–2 days before)**
> Hi! Our park BBQ is this [Day], [Time], at [Park] near [landmark]. Parking can fill up — carpool if
> you can! Don't forget your chairs. See you there ☀️

**Thank-you (after)**
> Thanks so much for joining the BBQ! 🍔 It was great seeing everyone. We'll share photos soon —
> thanks for bringing such good food and good vibes!

**Feedback request (optional)**
> Hope you had fun! Quick one: was the spot/timing good? Anything you'd change for the next one? 🙌

## F. Upgrade path
**This is very doable yourself.** Think about a professional organizer if: the group grows past ~50,
you want full catering and setup/cleanup handled, or it's a company/community picnic with logistics you'd
rather hand off. **Turn this plan into a request in one tap** and certified local organizers can quote
on it — your plan stays yours.

# Example 3 — Community Meetup for New Residents

## A. What you told us
- **Planning:** Community meetup (welcoming new residents / newcomers)
- **Guests:** ~40 attendees (families, mixed backgrounds)
- **Budget:** around $300
- **Location:** Community hall (indoors)
- **Special requirements:** **Multiple languages**, families with kids, **accessibility**, warm and
  welcoming to first-timers

## B. Your Activity Plan

**Summary**
A 2-hour welcoming meetup for ~40 newcomers and neighbors in a community hall. The plan focuses on making
first-timers feel comfortable: easy check-in with name tags, a short friendly welcome, light
refreshments, a simple icebreaker, a kids' corner, and clear multilingual, accessible details.

**Timeline**
- **2–3 weeks before:** book the hall (confirm **accessibility**), set date/time, spread the word
  through local/community channels in **multiple languages**.
- **1 week before:** estimate turnout, plan refreshments (incl. dietary/cultural options), prepare name
  tags & simple signage, line up 1–2 volunteers and any translators.
- **1–2 days before:** shop, print signage/name tags, prep the kids' corner, test mic/AV.
- **Day of:** set up, greet warmly, run the welcome + icebreaker, mingle, wind down, tidy up.

**Preparation tasks (checklist)**
- ☐ Book an **accessible** hall (step-free access, accessible restroom)
- ☐ Confirm date/time (early evening or weekend afternoon suits families)
- ☐ Promote in **several languages** through community groups
- ☐ Plan light refreshments with **dietary & cultural options** (veg, halal, etc.)
- ☐ Prepare name tags, simple multilingual signage, a welcome sheet of local info
- ☐ Set up a small **kids' corner** (mats, a few toys/crafts)
- ☐ Arrange a mic/speaker; recruit 1–2 friendly volunteers (and translators if possible)

**Day-of tasks**
- ☐ Set up check-in (name tags + a warm greeter)
- ☐ Lay out refreshments with clear labels
- ☐ Give a short, warm welcome; keep it simple and inclusive
- ☐ Run one easy icebreaker so people connect
- ☐ Keep an eye on the kids' corner
- ☐ Share local resources / next-meetup info before people leave
- ☐ Pack down and leave the hall clean

**After-event tasks**
- ☐ Return the hall / settle the booking
- ☐ Thank attendees & volunteers; share a simple "stay in touch" note
- ☐ Gather quick feedback for the next one

## C. Budget estimate (illustrative)
| | Low | Likely | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total | **$150** | **$300** | **$550** |

**Key cost drivers:** hall hire · light refreshments · printing (name tags, signage, welcome sheets) ·
kids' corner supplies · optional AV/mic rental. *A free/community-donated space moves you toward the low
end; a larger catered spread + AV pushes the high end.*

## D. Risk reminders
- **Attendance:** first-time community turnout is hard to predict — promote widely, ask for a soft RSVP,
  and cater flexibly so you're covered either way.
- **Food:** offer **vegetarian and culturally-appropriate (e.g., halal) options**, label everything
  clearly, and ask about allergies on sign-up.
- **Accessibility:** confirm step-free access and an accessible restroom; make signage clear and
  multilingual.
- **Children:** a supervised kids' corner keeps families comfortable and present.
- **Transportation:** include **public-transit directions** and nearby parking in every announcement;
  many newcomers won't drive.
- **Language:** have key info in multiple languages and, if possible, a couple of bilingual volunteers.

## E. Ready-to-send messages
**Invitation**
> 👋 Welcome Meetup for New Neighbors!
> A friendly, free-to-attend gathering for newcomers and locals.
> When: [Date], [Time]–[Time] · Where: [Hall] (step-free access) · Getting there: [transit + parking]
> Bring the family — there's a kids' corner, light refreshments (veg & halal options), and friendly
> faces. All languages welcome! Let us know you're coming: [contact].

**Reminder (1–2 days before)**
> Hello! Our welcome meetup is this [Day], [Time], at [Hall]. It's relaxed and family-friendly — just
> come as you are. Directions: [transit/parking]. Hope to see you there! 👋

**Thank-you (after)**
> Thank you for coming to the welcome meetup! 💛 It was wonderful to meet you. We'd love to keep in
> touch — here's how to hear about the next one: [link/contact]. You're always welcome here.

**Feedback request (optional)**
> Thanks again for joining! Quick question: did you feel welcome, and was the time/place easy for you?
> Anything we could do better next time? 🙏

## F. Upgrade path
**You can run this yourself** with a volunteer or two. Consider a professional organizer if: this grows
into a **regular series**, turnout climbs past ~75–100, or you want a bigger welcome festival with
catering, programming, and logistics handled. **One tap turns this plan into a request**, and certified
community organizers can respond with quotes — your plan stays yours.

## Is it worth paying for? (evaluation notes)

Across all three, a paying customer receives, in minutes, what would otherwise take hours of searching
and worrying:
- a **clear plan and timeline** tailored to their event,
- a **realistic budget range** with the levers to adjust it,
- the **safety/logistics reminders** people most often forget (allergies, supervision, weather, access),
- **ready-to-send messages** they'd otherwise agonize over, and
- a **safety net**: if it's bigger than a DIY job, one tap brings in a professional.

That bundle — plan + budget + reminders + messages + an escape hatch to a pro — is the value the
Activity Planner sells.
