# Organizer Academy — Module & Lesson Architecture v1

> **Purpose:** expand `ORGANIZER_ACADEMY_CURRICULUM_V1.md` into a complete **module + lesson**
> structure. **Learning architecture only — no lesson content, scripts, videos, or exam questions.**
> **Philosophy (PADI-inspired):** the goal is **not** to produce event planners — it is to produce
> **trustworthy organizers** who consistently deliver **safe, reliable, successful** real-world
> activities. Mastery-based, sequential, performance-driven; prevention and safety at the core.
> **Aligned with:** `ORGANIZER_ACADEMY_CURRICULUM_V1.md`, `MASTER_PRODUCT_DECISIONS.md` (§3, §10, §11),
> `ACTIVLIFEHUB_ORGANIZER_PHILOSOPHY_V1.md`.
> **Date:** 2026-06-06

*Per-lesson format:* **Lesson Name · Purpose · Learning Outcome** (outcome = what the organizer can *do*).

---

## Program Structure

- **Total modules:** 8
- **Total lessons:** 45
- **Recommended progression:** linear, Module 1 → Module 8 (each builds on the last).
- **Required completion order:** **sequential and gated.** A module's knowledge check must be passed
  before the next module unlocks. Two hard gates:
  1. **Module 1** must be completed first (orientation + mission/philosophy/standard).
  2. **Module 7 (Safety & Responsibility)** requires **mastery of all its mandatory lessons** before
     Module 8 and graduation — no exceptions, regardless of performance elsewhere.
- **Two entry routes (per curriculum §1):** *Beginner Course* runs every module in order; *Experienced
  Fast-Track* demonstrates equivalent competence against the same checkpoints and the same Module 7
  mastery requirement.

## Module 1 — Introduction to ActivLife Hub

- **1.1 Why Organizers Matter** · *Purpose:* establish the human importance of organizers. ·
  *Outcome:* can explain how organizers create communities, friendships, and experiences.
- **1.2 The World Needs More Organizers** · *Purpose:* introduce the rallying idea. · *Outcome:* can
  articulate why the world needs more organizers and where they fit in.
- **1.3 The ActivLife Hub Mission** · *Purpose:* present the official mission (F1). · *Outcome:* can
  state the mission and how it guides the platform.
- **1.4 The ActivLife Hub Philosophy** · *Purpose:* present "people are the hero; we help" (F2). ·
  *Outcome:* can explain the human-as-hero principle and what it means for an organizer.
- **1.5 The Organizer Philosophy** · *Purpose:* introduce the Organizer Standard (the 11 principles). ·
  *Outcome:* can name the core principles they will be held to (responsibility, prevention, safety, trust).
- **1.6 Platform Overview** · *Purpose:* map the ecosystem. · *Outcome:* can describe the three loops
  (Activity Planner · Academy · Organizer Platform) and how they connect.

## Module 2 — Working Inside ActivLife Hub

- **2.1 The Marketplace & Activities** · *Purpose:* understand supply-side discovery. · *Outcome:* can
  navigate the marketplace and explain how activities are listed and found.
- **2.2 Event Requests** · *Purpose:* understand demand-side opportunities. · *Outcome:* can read an
  Event Request and judge whether to respond.
- **2.3 Organizer Profiles & Followers** · *Purpose:* build presence and audience. · *Outcome:* can set
  up a credible profile and explain how followers/audience grow.
- **2.4 Reviews & Reputation** · *Purpose:* understand the trust engine. · *Outcome:* can explain how
  reviews work and how reputation is earned and lost.
- **2.5 Certification & Subscription** · *Purpose:* understand access and operating model. · *Outcome:*
  can explain what certification unlocks and how the organizer subscription works.
- **2.6 Verified vs Certified** · *Purpose:* clarify the two badges (Master §3). · *Outcome:* can
  distinguish Verified (identity) from Certified (skill) and why both build trust.

## Module 3 — Using OPE

- **3.1 OPE Planning & Scenarios** · *Purpose:* use OPE to start any plan. · *Outcome:* can turn a
  scenario into a structured draft plan in OPE.
- **3.2 Timelines & Checklists** · *Purpose:* sequence the work. · *Outcome:* can produce a usable
  timeline and task checklist from a plan.
- **3.3 Budgets & Estimates** · *Purpose:* cost an event credibly. · *Outcome:* can generate and read a
  low/likely/high budget estimate and explain it.
- **3.4 Risks in OPE** · *Purpose:* surface risk early. · *Outcome:* can identify and record the key
  risks OPE highlights for a plan.
- **3.5 Proposal Generation** · *Purpose:* produce the primary OPE outcome. · *Outcome:* can generate a
  professional client proposal (summary, timeline, plan, budget, risks) from a plan.

## Module 4 — Fundamentals of Organizing

- **4.1 The Event Lifecycle** · *Purpose:* see the whole arc. · *Outcome:* can describe an event from
  brief to wrap-up.
- **4.2 The Planning Process** · *Purpose:* the craft beneath the tool. · *Outcome:* can break an event
  into phases and a working plan without relying solely on OPE.
- **4.3 Resource Management** · *Purpose:* know what an event needs. · *Outcome:* can list and size the
  resources (people, equipment, venue, services) an event requires.
- **4.4 Dependencies & Sequencing** · *Purpose:* order the work correctly. · *Outcome:* can identify
  dependencies and sequence tasks so nothing blocks delivery.
- **4.5 Execution** · *Purpose:* run the day. · *Outcome:* can describe how to execute a plan on the day
  and keep it on track.

## Module 5 — Working with Participants

- **5.1 Communication** · *Purpose:* professional contact. · *Outcome:* can communicate clearly and
  promptly with participants/clients.
- **5.2 Setting Expectations** · *Purpose:* prevent disappointment. · *Outcome:* can set and confirm
  clear expectations before an event.
- **5.3 Difficult Situations** · *Purpose:* handle conflict calmly. · *Outcome:* can de-escalate and
  resolve common conflicts professionally.
- **5.4 Earning Reviews** · *Purpose:* convert delivery into reputation. · *Outcome:* can request and
  earn honest, positive reviews.
- **5.5 Community Building** · *Purpose:* think beyond one event. · *Outcome:* can describe how to turn
  participants into a returning community.

## Module 6 — Working with Vendors

- **6.1 Venues** · *Purpose:* secure the right space. · *Outcome:* can assess and book a venue against an
  event's needs.
- **6.2 Contractors & Service Providers** · *Purpose:* assemble the supply chain. · *Outcome:* can
  identify, brief, and compare contractors and service providers.
- **6.3 Negotiation Basics** · *Purpose:* fair, clear deals. · *Outcome:* can negotiate scope and terms
  professionally and reconcile them to the budget.
- **6.4 Documentation & Agreements** · *Purpose:* avoid misunderstandings. · *Outcome:* can capture what
  was agreed (scope, timing, cost) so vendors and plan stay aligned.

## Module 7 — Safety and Responsibility  *(most important module — all lessons MANDATORY)*

Every lesson below is **mandatory** and **mastery-required**; Module 7 is the hard gate before Module 8
and graduation.

- **7.1 Risk Awareness** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* see risk before it arrives. · *Outcome:* can run a
  basic risk assessment for any activity.
- **7.2 Prevention Over Recovery** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* the core safety mindset. · *Outcome:*
  can design an event to prevent problems rather than react to them.
- **7.3 Working with Children** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* heightened duty of care. · *Outcome:* can
  apply appropriate safeguards for activities involving children.
- **7.4 Sports Activities** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* physical-activity risk. · *Outcome:* can
  identify and mitigate sports-related risks.
- **7.5 Outdoor Activities** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* environment & weather risk. · *Outcome:* can
  plan for outdoor hazards and weather contingencies.
- **7.6 Water Activities** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* highest-duty setting. · *Outcome:* can apply
  the safeguards required for water-based activities.
- **7.7 Transportation** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* moving people safely. · *Outcome:* can plan safe,
  reliable transport for participants.
- **7.8 Emergency Thinking** · *(mandatory)* · *Purpose:* prepare for the rare case. · *Outcome:* can
  prepare an emergency plan and know when/how to act if prevention fails.

## Module 8 — Building an Organizer Career

- **8.1 Winning Your First Client** · *Purpose:* convert capability into opportunity. · *Outcome:* can
  respond to a request and present a winning proposal.
- **8.2 Delivering Your First Event** · *Purpose:* execute under real stakes. · *Outcome:* can deliver a
  first event to standard.
- **8.3 First Income** · *Purpose:* close the loop. · *Outcome:* can complete the booking/payment flow
  and get paid correctly.
- **8.4 Reputation & Reviews** · *Purpose:* compound trust. · *Outcome:* can build a reputation that
  attracts the next client.
- **8.5 Repeat Clients** · *Purpose:* sustainable work. · *Outcome:* can turn a one-off into repeat
  business.
- **8.6 Long-Term Growth** · *Purpose:* a real career. · *Outcome:* can plan toward steady, growing
  income as an organizer.

## Knowledge Checks

- **End-of-module knowledge check** after every module (formative, mastery-based — the gate to unlock
  the next module).
- **Per-lesson micro-checks** within **Module 7** (each mandatory safety lesson is individually
  checked, not just the module).
- **Cumulative readiness check** before graduation, drawing across all modules (feeds the final exam).
- Knowledge checks are **mastery-gated**, not time-gated (PADI principle): progress on demonstrated
  competence, not hours spent.

## Practical Assignments

One practical per module (real-world application, not theory):
- **M1:** write a short "why I want to organize" + map the three-loop ecosystem.
- **M2:** set up a complete organizer profile and interpret a sample Event Request.
- **M3:** produce a full sample **plan + client proposal** in OPE.
- **M4:** build a phase + dependency plan (without OPE) for a given brief.
- **M5:** draft a participant communication and resolve a difficult-situation scenario.
- **M6:** assemble a vendor shortlist with briefs and a simple agreement outline for a brief.
- **M7 (mandatory practical):** complete a **risk assessment + prevention plan** for a higher-duty
  activity (children / sports / outdoor / water / transport).
- **M8:** build a go-to-market plan tracing First Client → First Event → First Income.

## Graduation Requirements

Before certification, the candidate must have:
1. **Completed all 8 modules in order** and passed every **end-of-module knowledge check**.
2. **Mastered Module 7** — all mandatory safety lessons + the **M7 practical** — regardless of other
   scores (non-negotiable gate).
3. **Completed all practical assignments**, including the **M3 OPE proposal** and the **M7 risk
   assessment**.
4. **Passed the Certification Exam** (knowledge + practical demonstration) per curriculum §4.
5. **Accepted the Organizer Standard** (Foundational F3 / philosophy #2–#8).

On meeting all five → the **Certified Organizer** credential (curriculum §7). The Fast-Track route must
satisfy the **same** requirements, especially the Module 7 mastery gate.

> **Framing (Master §10.4):** graduation is the milestone that opens the door — the goal beyond it is
> the organizer's first client, first event, and first income.
