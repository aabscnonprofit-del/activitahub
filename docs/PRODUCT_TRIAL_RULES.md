# Product Trial Rules

> **Status: MANDATORY (process/governance).** These rules apply to **every Product Trial** in ActivLife Hub.
> A Product Trial is an evidence-based investigation — the read-only research step that reduces uncertainty
> before a product decision. This document governs *how a trial is conducted and reported*; it defines no
> product behavior and introduces no product feature. It complements `ENGINEERING_PROCESS.md` and does not
> override the Constitution or Product Canon.

---

## Purpose

A Product Trial exists to **reduce uncertainty**.

- It does **not** exist to invent new ideas.
- It does **not** exist to confirm existing beliefs.
- It exists to determine **what the available evidence actually supports**.

---

## Possible outcomes

Every Product Trial is **successful** if it produces one of the following outcomes:

1. The evidence **supports the current industry approach**.
2. The evidence shows that **no stable industry approach exists**.
3. The evidence reveals a **better-supported alternative**.
4. The available evidence is **insufficient to reach a conclusion**.

**All four outcomes are equally valuable.**

---

## Important

- Do **not** optimize.
- Do **not** redesign.
- Do **not** propose ActivLife Hub features unless explicitly requested.
- Do **not** assume that a different solution is automatically better.

**Innovation is not the objective. Understanding is the objective.**

---

## Standard solutions

If the evidence demonstrates that an existing industry solution is well supported, **report that conclusion
clearly**.

Do **not** reject a standard solution merely because it is common. A standard solution that is strongly
supported by evidence is a **valuable research result**.

---

## New solutions

If the evidence demonstrates that existing approaches leave important problems unsolved, **identify those
problems**.

Do **not** invent a replacement unless explicitly requested.

---

## Deliverable

Every Product Trial must end with:

- **Evidence**
- **Verdict**
- **Confidence level**

The verdict must be supported by **evidence rather than preference**.
