# Professional Baseline Principle

> **Status: AUTHORITATIVE Product Principle.** A long-lived product requirement that guides future
> development. Not architecture, not implementation. Indexed in `PRODUCT_PRINCIPLES_INDEX.md`.

## Principle

> **ActivLife Hub must provide the complete operational baseline expected by professional event organizers.**

The platform should become a **replacement** for the operational software an organizer runs their business
on — not an additional application they open alongside everything else. If an organizer still needs another
tool to run the operational side of their business, the baseline is incomplete.

ActivLife Hub's unique AI capabilities are built **on top of** this baseline, **never instead of it**. The AI
that understands intent, plans, decomposes, costs, and coordinates is the differentiator — but it does not
excuse the absence of the ordinary operational capabilities a professional expects. A brilliant AI on a
missing baseline is still not a tool a professional can run their business on.

## The baseline (not exhaustive)

The minimum operational capabilities a professional event organizer expects:

- CRM
- Client communication
- Contracts
- Proposals
- Estimates / Quotes
- Invoices
- Payments
- Documents
- Scheduling
- Project history
- Team collaboration
- Files & attachments

This list is a floor, not a ceiling; the market's expected baseline may grow over time.

## Scope of this principle

This document **intentionally does not compare ActivLife Hub with any specific competitor or product.** It
states the **minimum professional operational capabilities expected by the market** — the baseline itself —
independently of who else provides it.

## The test

For any operational area, ask: *would a professional organizer still need to leave ActivLife Hub to do this?*
If yes, the baseline is not yet met there. The AI layer is judged separately: it must **add** capability on
top of a complete baseline, not substitute for a missing one.
