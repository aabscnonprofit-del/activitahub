**How to present it publicly:** as a short, dignified **manifesto** (the cultural foundation), readable

plain, no marketing hype.

trust-and-safety ones most meaningful to the public —

# Pricing Page

**What products exist (the three paid loops):**
1. **Activity Planner** — for users planning their own event.
2. **Organizer Academy / Certification** — to learn and earn the credential.
3. **Organizer Platform (subscription)** — to operate, win clients, and use OPE professionally.

**What should be explained:**
- That **each layer is a paid product** (value-first; what each unlocks).
- What you get at each level and why it's worth it (capability, credential, clients, OPE).
- That certification is an investment in a profession, not a free trial.

**What should NOT be explained yet:**
- **Final prices are not settled** — the $9.99 vs $29 reconciliation is open (P11); don't publish a
  number that may change. Show the **products and value**, defer the figure until decided.
- **Verified Organizer fee** mechanics/amount (undecided).
- **OPE usage quotas / "deep plan"** policy (undecided).
- **Future lead/proposal fees, commission, specialty/master tiers** — not now.

> Pricing copy must align with the all-paid principle and must **not** reintroduce "free."

# Homepage Funnel

How a new visitor should move through the site:

**User path (demand):**
Land on hero → "Explore activities" (or "Plan an Event") → browse marketplace / start a plan →
join or plan an event. Along the way, the Mission band and Become-an-Organizer block plant the seed
that they could *become* an organizer.

**Organizer path (supply):**
Land on hero → Mission band resonates → "Become an organizer" → Become-an-Organizer page (income + path
+ OPE) → Academy page (learn + certify) → onboarding → certification → Organizer Platform → win clients
and earn.

**Convergence:** both paths meet in the marketplace — users create demand, certified organizers supply
it. The homepage's job is to send each visitor confidently down the right path while making the *other*
path visible.
