# Organizer Academy — Curriculum Architecture v1

> **Purpose:** the complete curriculum **structure** for the ActivLife Hub Organizer Academy.
> **Audience:** a person with **no professional organizing experience** who wants additional income,
> a new profession, the ability to organize activities professionally, and success inside the
> ActivLife Hub ecosystem.
> **Type:** curriculum architecture only — **no lesson content, no exam questions.**
> **Aligned with:** `MASTER_PRODUCT_DECISIONS.md` (§3, §10, §11), `BRAND_FOUNDATION_AND_PLATFORM_STRUCTURE.md`,
> `ORGANIZER_CAREER_PATH_V1.md`, `OPE_V1_TECHNICAL_DESIGN.md`.
> **Date:** 2026-06-06

**PADI-inspired design philosophy** (how it shapes this curriculum):
- **Modular & sequential** — small, ordered modules; master one before the next.
- **Performance-based** — objectives are "the organizer can *do* X," not "knows about X."
- **Theory → practice → real** — knowledge development → a practical/simulated task → a real first
  event, mirroring PADI's knowledge-development → confined → open-water progression.
- **Standards, safety, responsibility, process** at the core — competence *and* duty of care.
- **Mastery learning** — knowledge-review checkpoints per module; progression gated on demonstrated
  competence, not time spent.

---

## 1. Program Overview

- **Purpose:** take a beginner from "I'd like to do this" to a **Certified Organizer** who can plan,
  cost, and deliver real activities professionally and operate successfully on ActivLife Hub —
  directly serving the mission, *The World Needs More Organizers* (Master §11.1).
- **Expected outcomes:** a graduate can produce a professional client proposal (via OPE), source and
  manage vendors, run an event safely and responsibly, meet ActivLife Hub standards, and win and keep
  clients — i.e., reach **First Client → First Event → First Income** (Master §10.4).
- **Certification path (two entry routes, one credential):**
  - **Beginner Course** — the full guided program (Modules 1–8) for newcomers.
  - **Experienced Fast-Track** — an assessment route for people who already have organizing
    experience; they demonstrate competence against the same standards instead of taking every module.
  - Both routes converge on the **Certification Exam + Organizer Standards** (§4–§5) → the
    **Certified Organizer** credential.
- **Format:** each module = knowledge development + a **knowledge-review checkpoint**; the program ends
  with a final exam and a **practical demonstration** (a sample plan/proposal produced with OPE).
- **Note (paid program, Master §11.8):** the Academy is a paid product; this document defines structure,
  not pricing.

## 2. Learning Objectives

A **graduate can:**
1. Plan an activity end-to-end — scope, timeline, resources, dependencies, and execution.
2. Use **OPE** to turn a scenario into a structured plan, budget estimate, and **client proposal**.
3. Produce a **defensible budget** and communicate pricing clearly and honestly.
4. **Communicate** with participants/clients, set expectations, and handle conflict professionally.
5. Source, brief, and **negotiate** with venues, contractors, and service providers.
6. Identify and manage **risk**, and uphold **safety and responsibility** (incl. children, sports,
   outdoor settings).
7. Operate the **ActivLife Hub ecosystem** — profile, marketplace, Event Requests, reviews,
   certification, subscription, payments.
8. Meet the **Organizer Standards** (§5) consistently.
9. Win a **first client**, deliver a **first event**, and build toward **repeat business and reputation**.

## 3. Module Structure

*Each module below lists: **Purpose · Topics · Performance outcome · Checkpoint.** Topics are the
syllabus scope, not lesson content.*

### Module 1 — Introduction to ActivLife Hub
- **Purpose:** ground the organizer in the mission and the world they're joining.
- **Topics:** Mission · Philosophy · "The World Needs More Organizers" · Platform ecosystem.
- **Performance outcome:** can explain the mission, the human-as-hero philosophy, and how the three
  loops (Activity Planner · Academy · Organizer Platform) fit together.
- **Checkpoint:** knowledge review (orientation).

### Module 2 — How ActivLife Hub Works
- **Purpose:** operational fluency in the platform.
- **Topics:** Marketplace · Activities · Event Requests · Organizer Profiles · Followers / audience ·
  Reviews · Certification · Subscription.
- **Performance outcome:** can navigate the marketplace, receive and read an Event Request, set up a
  profile, and explain how certification, subscription, reviews, and payments work.
- **Checkpoint:** knowledge review + a guided profile-setup task.

### Module 3 — Using OPE
- **Purpose:** make the beginner immediately capable with the platform's core tool.
- **Topics:** Planning · Timelines · Checklists · Budgets · Scenarios · Risk management (within OPE).
- **Performance outcome:** can take a scenario through OPE to a plan, timeline, checklist, budget
  range, and a draft client proposal.
- **Checkpoint:** practical task — produce a sample plan + budget with OPE.

### Module 4 — Fundamentals of Event Planning
- **Purpose:** the craft beneath the tool.
- **Topics:** Planning process · Resources · Dependencies · Execution.
- **Performance outcome:** can decompose any event into phases, resources, and dependencies and
  sequence them into an executable plan.
- **Checkpoint:** practical task — build a phase/dependency plan for a given brief.

### Module 5 — Working with Participants
- **Purpose:** client/participant relationship skills.
- **Topics:** Communication · Expectations · Conflict handling · Reviews.
- **Performance outcome:** can set clear expectations, communicate professionally, de-escalate
  conflict, and earn good reviews.
- **Checkpoint:** scenario-based knowledge review (communication/conflict).

### Module 6 — Working with Vendors
- **Purpose:** supply-side competence.
- **Topics:** Venues · Contractors · Service providers · Negotiation basics.
- **Performance outcome:** can identify, brief, compare, and negotiate with vendors, and reconcile
  vendor scope against the plan/budget.
- **Checkpoint:** practical task — assemble a vendor list + briefs for a brief.

### Module 7 — Trust, Safety and Responsibility
- **Purpose:** duty of care — the non-negotiable core (PADI-style standards/safety emphasis).
- **Topics:** Children · Sports · Outdoor activities · Risk awareness · Organizer responsibilities.
- **Performance outcome:** can assess risk for higher-duty settings, apply appropriate safeguards, and
  state the organizer's legal/ethical responsibilities and platform standards.
- **Checkpoint:** knowledge review (mastery-required; see §4 — this is a critical-safety module).

### Module 8 — Building an Organizer Career
- **Purpose:** convert competence into a livelihood.
- **Topics:** First client · First event · Repeat clients · Reputation · Long-term growth.
- **Performance outcome:** can win a first client, deliver a first event, request reviews, and plan
  toward repeat business and a growing reputation.
- **Checkpoint:** knowledge review + a personal go-to-market plan.

## 4. Certification Exam

*(Structure only — no questions written here.)*
- **Exam structure:** a **knowledge exam** spanning all modules, plus a **practical demonstration**
  (produce a complete sample plan + client proposal via OPE to standard). Module knowledge-reviews act
  as formative checkpoints feeding the final exam.
- **Pass criteria (recommended):** an overall knowledge threshold (recommend ~80%), **and** mastery of
  the **critical-safety module (Module 7)** required regardless of overall score, **and** a passing
  practical demonstration. Competence on safety is non-negotiable.
- **Retake policy (recommended):** targeted **remediation** on failed areas, then a retake after a
  short review/cooldown; a sensible cap on consecutive attempts before required re-study; the Fast-Track
  route follows the same standards and retake rules.

## 5. Organizer Standards

The behavioral and operational standards every Certified Organizer agrees to uphold (PADI-style: the
credential certifies *adherence to standards*, not just knowledge):

1. **Duty of care & safety** — assess and mitigate risk; meet heightened obligations for children,
   sports, and outdoor activities.
2. **Honesty in proposals & pricing** — accurate scope, transparent estimates, no hidden costs.
3. **Reliability** — show up, deliver what was promised, communicate changes promptly.
4. **Professional communication** — responsive, respectful, clear expectations.
5. **Compliance** — required licenses, permits, and insurance where applicable.
6. **Fair cancellation & refund conduct** — honor platform rules and treat clients fairly.
7. **Privacy & respect** — handle participant information responsibly.
8. **Code of conduct** — inclusive, safe, welcoming activities; no harassment or discrimination.
9. **Good standing** — maintain reviews and resolve issues; repeated violations risk the credential.

## 6. Certification Levels

**Recommendation for v1: two operational levels + one orthogonal trust badge.**
- **Organizer** — enrolled / in-training (Academy started, not yet certified). May learn and *practice*
  but not yet sell to clients.
- **Certified Organizer** — passed the exam + standards; the credential clients trust; unlocks the
  professional layer (gated by active subscription, Master §4).
- **Verified Organizer** *(orthogonal, stackable)* — an **identity** badge (Master §3), independent of
  skill; can be held alongside Certified.

**Reasoning:**
- Master §3 already defines **Certified (skill)** and **Verified (identity)** as distinct, stackable
  badges — the curriculum should match, not invent a parallel scheme.
- Master §9 warns against overbuilding — ship the **minimal credible ladder** (Organizer → Certified)
  first; a deep PADI-style ladder before there's a market is premature.
- **Design the architecture to allow growth, but don't ship it yet.** Recommended **future** levels
  (post-v1), explicitly out of scope now:
  - **Specialty certifications** — category endorsements (e.g., Weddings, Outdoor, Kids), mapping
    naturally onto the OPE knowledge-base categories (the Wedding KB is the template).
  - **Master / Mentor Organizer** — a senior tier for high-volume, high-reputation organizers who can
    mentor newcomers (a PADI "Divemaster/Instructor" analogue).
- Net: **v1 = Organizer + Certified Organizer (+ Verified)**, with a clear path to specialties and a
  master tier later.

## 7. Academy Completion Outcomes

On passing certification + standards, the graduate gains:
- The **Certified Organizer** credential — a **verifiable certificate** and public badge on their
  organizer profile (`/o/<slug>`).
- Access to the **Organizer Platform** (professional layer), subject to an **active subscription**
  (Master §4): full **OPE** professional output, **client proposals**, answering **Event Requests**,
  and **earning** through proposal → booking → payment.
- A **public organizer profile** that builds reputation via reviews and followers/audience.
- Eligibility for **future specialty certifications** and the **Master Organizer** path (when launched).

**Framing (Master §10.4):** certification is the **milestone that opens the door** — the goal beyond it
is the organizer's **first client, first event, first income**, then repeat business and a real career.
