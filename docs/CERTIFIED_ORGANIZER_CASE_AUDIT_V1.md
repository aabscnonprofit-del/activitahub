# Certified Organizer Exam — Case Audit v1

**Source:** CERTIFIED_ORGANIZER_EXAM_CASES_V1 (31 draft cases)

Audit only. No exam questions, no scoring, no program redesign. Every case is
judged against six checks and given a verdict: **Keep / Revise / Remove**.

**The six checks (markers used per case):**
1. **Judgment or memory** — does it test thinking, or recall of a "right word"/required field?
2. **Multiple reasonable solutions** — can two strong candidates answer differently and both be right?
3. **Biased to one answer** — is it rigged toward an author-preferred answer? (Note: a *safety/ethics floor* having one defensible answer is **not** bias — it's a legitimate gate.)
4. **Real situation** — would this actually happen to an organizer?
5. **Distinguishes strong from weak** — discrimination power (high / moderate / low).
6. **Verdict.**

A recurring distinction below: **judgment cases** (open, multiple valid answers,
high discrimination) vs **gate cases** (safety/ethics floors — one defensible
answer, low discrimination, but high stakes; they exist to *exclude*, not to
*rank*). Both are valid; the exam needs the right mix of each.

### Missing scenario categories
The bank covers its eleven required themes but has gaps a real organizer hits:
1. **Safety vs. client pressure** — client asks to cut a safety/access corner to save money (a *judgment* safety case, not hazard-spotting). The safety section is all recognition; none force a values trade-off.
2. **Cultural / religious requirements** — dietary law, prayer needs, gender considerations, mixed-faith guest lists.
3. **Money disputes after the fact** — cancellation, partial refund, "it wasn't what I paid for."
4. **Scope creep / boundaries** — client keeps adding requests; when and how to hold the line.
5. **Participant behavior on the day** — an intoxicated or disruptive guest, conflict between attendees.
6. **Organizer capacity / saying no** — being asked to take an event they can't realistically deliver well.
7. **Mixed-population events** — children *and* seniors at the same event with competing needs (current cases isolate each group).
8. **Non-physical inclusion** — a neurodiverse participant, a language barrier, sensory needs.
9. **Participant privacy** — handling attendees' contact info and personal data appropriately.

### Recommended final case bank size
Target **~40 cases (4–5 per section A–I)**:
- Keep 25, revise 6 (→ ~5 after the 12/13 merge) = **~30 retained**.
- Add **~10–12 new** cases to cover the missing categories above, weighted toward
  *judgment* cases (multiple valid answers) and the one missing *safety-under-pressure* type.
- Aim for a deliberate mix per section: mostly **judgment cases** (to rank skill)
  plus a small number of **gate cases** (safety/ethics floors, to exclude) — roughly
  3–4 judgment to 1 gate. The current draft is gate-heavy in Section F; rebalance there.

This keeps the exam focused on professional judgment — discovery, reasoning,
risk and client understanding, concept and planning judgment, and ethics — and
away from memory, terminology, or guessing the author's preferred answer.
